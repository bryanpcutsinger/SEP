How to Read This Report
What is the SEP?
The Summary of Economic Projections is released four times a year alongside FOMC meeting minutes. Each of the 19 FOMC participants submits projections for key economic variables.
Median
The middle projection when all participants' forecasts are ranked from lowest to highest.
Central Tendency
The range of projections after excluding the three highest and three lowest — a measure of where most participants cluster.
Range
The full spread from the lowest to the highest projection across all participants.
Dot Plot
A chart showing each participant's projection for the federal funds rate. Each dot is one participant — more dots at a level means more agreement.
PCE vs. CPI
The Fed targets PCE (Personal Consumption Expenditures) inflation, not the more widely reported CPI. PCE tends to run slightly below CPI.
Longer Run
Participants' estimates of where each variable will settle in the long term, once temporary shocks have faded. Think of it as the economy's equilibrium.
